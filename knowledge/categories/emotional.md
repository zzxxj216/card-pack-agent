# Category Playbook: Emotional

**L1**: `emotional`
**Status**: v0.1 — Initial English draft. Zero data.
**Validation progress**: 0 / 30 packs

---

## 1. Scope

### 1.1 In scope
- Packs anchored on an **emotion / psychological state** as the core,
  independent of a specific external trigger
- Common emotional anchors:
  - Loneliness (especially big-city / post-college / late-20s)
  - Burnout (work, caregiving, parenting)
  - Imposter / comparison exhaustion (social media fatigue)
  - Grief at low intensity (ambient loss, missing a past self)
  - Quiet joy / gratitude / contentment
  - Anxiety / overthinking loops
  - Healing / recovery / post-therapy realizations
  - Nostalgia for past selves / past eras

### 1.2 Out of scope
- An emotion triggered by a **specific holiday** → `festival`
- An emotion triggered by a **specific news event** → `trending_event`
- "How to overcome X emotion" step-by-step → `knowledge`
- Personal transformation stories → `growth`
- Relational emotion (between specific people) → `relationship`

### 1.3 Intensity range
Stay below the saturation line (see `global_style_guide.md §5`). Emotional
packs easily tip into over-emotion, which both the Evaluator flags and
audiences reject as performative.

## 2. Success patterns

### 2.1 Resonance + healing (`resonance_healing`) — primary mechanism

**When to use**: default choice for this L1; the whole point is "you are
not alone in this"

**Visual recipe**:
- Cool or neutral palette (navy, soft green, dusk) for solitude themes
- Warm palette for healing / quiet-joy themes
- Single-object subjects: a half-drunk coffee, a desk lamp at 11pm, an
  unmade bed, a phone face-down
- Soft light, shallow depth of field
- Lots of negative space — at least 40% on average

**Copy recipe**:
- Second person "you"
- Short observational lines: "you told them you were fine three times today"
- Avoid diagnosing ("this is anxiety"); describe the thing
- No "I'm here for you" — reads condescending
- Hook <= 8 words; body <= 12

**Pacing (50 cards)**:
- 1-3: specific behavioral detail + unmistakable recognition
  ("you closed the app and opened it again")
- 4-15: detail expansion (other small behaviors)
- 16-30: widening (context, why)
- 31-42: soft reframe ("you are not the problem you think you are")
- 43-50: quiet landing, optional soft CTA

### 2.2 Regret sting (`regret_sting`) — use sparingly

**When to use**: missing-a-past-self packs, "I should have been gentler with
her" packs

**Visual recipe**:
- Aged-film look, low saturation
- Empty-scene objects
- Avoid direct human face

**Copy**: temporal dissonance, short lines, no uplift

### 2.3 Contrast twist (`contrast_twist`)

**When to use**: the emotion looks like A but is actually B
  ("it looks like burnout but it's grief")

**Visual recipe**: half a, half b, strong reversal card

**Copy**: setup must earn the reversal; don't shortcut it

### 2.4 Aphorism / lesson (`aphorism_lesson`)

**When to use**: minimally; risk of preachy tone is very high for this L1.
If used, ground every aphorism in a concrete observation.

## 3. Mechanisms to avoid on this L1

- `blessing_ritual` — reads saccharine without a holiday anchor
- `utility_share` — converts to `knowledge` L1 the moment you list steps
- `conflict_tension` — possible but narrow; only when the tension is
  internal (self-vs-expectation), not external

---

## 4. Emotional-specific taboos

1. **No diagnosis language** ("this is depression", "you have anxiety") —
   we describe, we don't clinically label
2. **No forced uplift**: don't end every pack with "you got this"
3. **Over-emotion ceiling** — hard rule: 3+ consecutive cards with
   "cry / tears / sobbing / broken / wrecked / devastated" flags the pack
4. **No pathologizing normal feelings**: being tired is not burnout; being
   sad is not depression
5. **No "top 10 signs you have X"** framing — that's `knowledge`, not
   `emotional`
6. **Avoid saturated openers**:
   - "We've all been there..."
   - "Every [20-something / woman / creator] knows this feeling..."
   - "POV: you're literally me"

---

## 5. Visual notes

- Single-person cards: favor back-of-head, hands, silhouette, partial frame
  — a direct portrait face shortens the "resonance" feeling because the
  viewer's brain parses it as "a different person"
- Scene cards: pick scenes with a built-in "pause" (a still mug, a window
  at dusk, a bus at night) over action scenes
- Color restraint: 2 colors + 1 accent is ideal; more fragments the feel
